We study zero-knowledge verifiable offline DQN training from committed trajectories. The goal is to let a verifier check that key offline reinforcement learning computations are carried out correctly on a pre-committed dataset without revealing the underlying transitions, rewards, or model weights.

Our current prototype focuses on a pre-ZK artifact/verifier setting rather than a full cryptographic proving backend. We first commit the offline dataset with a Merkle-style data-commitment pipeline, then export verification artifacts for committed transition membership, Bellman target correctness, TD-loss correctness, and checkpoint anchoring. We further extend this prototype to a stronger one-step update statement that checks gradient recomputation, parameter-delta consistency, SGD update consistency, and target-network invariance for a committed minibatch.

Beyond isolated one-step verification, we also evaluate short verified training traces by chaining consecutive verified update steps with explicit checkpoint consistency and target-network synchronization semantics. Our current experiments show successful verification for multiple committed minibatch sizes in the one-step setting and for longer short traces up to 8 steps in the multi-step setting.

These results should be interpreted as preliminary pre-ZK consistency results rather than end-to-end zero-knowledge proof-of-training results. Nevertheless, they provide evidence that offline DQN training on committed trajectories can be structured into verifiable statements that move from TD arithmetic, to one-step update correctness, and further toward short process-level training-trace verification.

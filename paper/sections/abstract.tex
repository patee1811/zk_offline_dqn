We study zero-knowledge verifiable offline DQN training from committed trajectories. The goal is to let a verifier check that key offline reinforcement-learning computations are carried out correctly on a pre-committed dataset without revealing the underlying transitions, rewards, or model weights.

Our current system is a pre-ZK artifact/verifier prototype rather than a full cryptographic proof-of-training backend. We first commit the offline dataset with a Merkle-style data-commitment pipeline, then export verification artifacts for committed transition membership, Bellman target correctness, TD-loss correctness, minibatch-loss consistency, and checkpoint anchoring. We further strengthen this prototype with a one-step update statement that checks gradient recomputation, parameter-delta consistency, SGD update consistency, and target-network invariance for a committed minibatch.

Beyond isolated one-step verification, we also evaluate short verified training traces by chaining consecutive verified update steps with explicit checkpoint consistency and target-network synchronization semantics. Our current benchmark supports successful verification for 2-step, 4-step, and 8-step traces. We additionally strengthen the 8-step setting with deterministic contiguous sampling-rule enforcement using public batch size and start offset, and we include a negative sanity check showing that the verifier rejects a tampered artifact whose public batch indices no longer match the declared rule.

These results should be interpreted as preliminary pre-ZK consistency results rather than end-to-end zero-knowledge proof-of-training results. Nevertheless, they provide evidence that offline DQN training on committed trajectories can be structured into meaningful verifiable statements that progress from TD arithmetic, to one-step update correctness, to short process-level training-trace verification under a stricter public sampling rule.

\section{Conclusion}

This paper presents a ZK-backed verification pipeline for temporal-difference
computation in offline DQN over committed trajectories. The contribution is
scoped deliberately. Rather than claiming full proof-of-training, we prove the
first RL-specific backend relation: committed transition membership plus
fixed-point Bellman target, TD error, SmoothL1 loss, and minibatch-average loss
consistency.

The implementation commits a CartPole transition dataset with a SHA-256 Merkle
tree, exports canonical TD witnesses, and proves TD-1/2/4/8 relations in an SP1
zkVM backend. The locked benchmark reports successful proof generation and
verification, proof sizes around 2.8 MB, verification time around 0.16 seconds,
and Python/SP1 agreement on valid and tampered fixtures. The negative suite
rejects schema, transition, Merkle, rounding, done-branch, target-value,
claimed-output, and batch-aggregation tampering.

The remaining path is clear. One-step update and short-trace verifiers already
exist as Python pre-ZK extensions, but they must be ported to a proof backend
before the project can claim proof-of-update or proof-of-training. Future work
should therefore prioritize neural-network forward proofs, argmax and target
network semantics, gradient and optimizer proofs, diverse minibatch fixtures,
and recursive aggregation for longer traces.

The main conclusion is that offline DQN training can be decomposed into
verifiable layers, and that the TD/minibatch-TD layer is already practical
enough to prove in a real zkVM backend over committed trajectory data.

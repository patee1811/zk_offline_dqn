We consider a discounted Markov decision process with state space $\states$,
action space $\actions$, reward function $r$, terminal flag $d$, and discount
factor $\gamma \in [0,1)$. The concrete benchmark uses
CartPole-v1~\cite{brockman2016gym}, whose observations are four-dimensional
continuous vectors and whose action space has two discrete actions.

\paragraph{Committed transition dataset.}
The offline dataset is fixed before verification:
\[
\dataset = \{(s_i,a_i,r_i,s'_i,d_i)\}_{i=1}^N .
\]
Each transition is serialized into a fixed-point integer leaf and committed in a
SHA-256 Merkle tree~\cite{merkle1987}. The public dataset commitment is the
Merkle root $h(\dataset)$. A prover may keep the transition contents private
while revealing enough authentication data to prove membership in the committed
dataset.

\paragraph{Offline DQN TD computation.}
For a DQN with online parameters $\theta$ and target parameters $\bar{\theta}$,
the Double-DQN target for transition $i$ is
\begin{align}
a_i^* &= \arg\max_{a' \in \actions} Q_\theta(s'_i,a'), \\
y_i &= r_i + \gamma (1-d_i) Q_{\bar{\theta}}(s'_i,a_i^*).
\end{align}
The TD error is
\[
\delta_i = Q_\theta(s_i,a_i) - y_i,
\]
and the SmoothL1 loss with $\beta=1$ is
\[
\ell(\delta_i) =
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{2}\delta_i^2 & \text{if } |\delta_i| < 1,\\
|\delta_i| - \frac{1}{2} & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]
For a minibatch $B$, the batch loss is the average of per-transition losses.

\paragraph{Backend-proved scope.}
The SP1 backend does not yet prove neural-network forward evaluation or the
argmax operation. Instead, the Q-values used in the Bellman target are witness
values, and the backend proves that the committed transition, target arithmetic,
TD error, loss, and claimed public outputs are mutually consistent. This is the
first backend target because it is small enough to prove today while still
capturing RL-specific semantics: terminal branches, Bellman backups, TD errors,
and replay membership.

\paragraph{Pre-ZK extension scope.}
The repository also contains Python verifiers for forward TD consistency,
one-step SGD update checks, and short trace chaining. These are useful for
engineering the future proof-of-update path, but they are not claimed as ZK
proofs in this paper. They are reported separately as pre-ZK extension evidence.

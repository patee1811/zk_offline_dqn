\paragraph{Offline RL and DQN.}
DQN combines value-function approximation, experience replay, and a target
network for temporal-difference learning~\cite{mnih2015dqn,watkins1992qlearning}.
Double DQN separates next-action selection from target-network value evaluation
to reduce overestimation~\cite{hasselt2016double}. Offline RL studies the fixed
dataset setting~\cite{levine2020offline}, where value learning can be brittle
under distribution shift~\cite{fujimoto2019offpolicy}; CQL addresses this with
conservative value regularization~\cite{kumar2020cql}. Our work does not
improve the RL algorithm. It makes selected DQN-style replay and TD computations
verifiable over committed data.

\paragraph{Proof-of-learning and proof-of-training.}
Proof-of-Learning introduced the problem of proving that a model resulted from
a claimed training procedure~\cite{jia2021proofoflearning}. zkDL and related
systems target zero-knowledge proofs for deep learning training, including
neural nonlinearities and backpropagation~\cite{zkdl2023}. Kaizen gives a
zero-knowledge proof-of-training construction for DNNs based on per-iteration
proofs and recursive composition~\cite{kaizen2024}. zkPoT work based on optimum
vicinity explores another way to make training claims succinct~\cite{zkpotvicinity2025}.
These systems motivate our goal, but they do not directly encode offline
DQN-specific semantics such as terminal Bellman branches, replay membership,
Double-DQN argmax, target-network selection, and TD losses.

\paragraph{Verifiable and zero-knowledge machine learning.}
Verifiable ML systems have heavily studied inference integrity. SafetyNets
builds proofs for neural-network inference on untrusted clouds~\cite{safetynets2017};
zkCNN proves CNN predictions and accuracy while hiding private information
~\cite{zkcnn2021}; VeriML gives integrity and payment assurances for outsourced
ML tasks~\cite{veriml2021}. Recent surveys organize this space into verifiable
inference, testing, and training~\cite{zkmlsurvey2026}. Our relation is
different: the first-class computation is a Bellman TD claim over committed
replay data rather than a supervised prediction.

\paragraph{zkVMs and proof backends.}
General-purpose zkVMs trade circuit specialization for implementation speed.
SP1 is a Rust-oriented zkVM used here because the relation combines hashing,
Merkle paths, schema checks, integer arithmetic, and branching
~\cite{succinct2024sp1}. RISC Zero is another zkVM-style backend in the same
design space; in this project it is treated as a comparison or future backend
rather than the main implementation target.

\paragraph{Verifiable RL.}
Prior verifiable RL work often focuses on interpretable or formally verifiable
policies rather than cryptographic verification of training computations.
Programmatic-policy approaches search for policies that can be checked by
symbolic methods~\cite{verma2019verifiable}. Our work targets a different
layer: cryptographic or deterministic verification of the replay membership,
Bellman arithmetic, model-grounded TD evidence, and update evidence inside
offline value learning.
